# 19 TAC §74.24 — Credit by Examination (key provisions)

Local reference copy of the **governing rule**. There is no longer an official
standalone PDF — TEA retired the per-chapter PDFs and the Texas Secretary of State now
serves the Texas Administrative Code as HTML only. The provisions below are quoted from
the authoritative full text; see the links at the bottom for the complete rule.

> **Governing statute:** Texas Education Code §§ 25.007 and 28.023.
> **Rule:** 19 Texas Administrative Code (TAC) §74.24, adopted by the State Board of Education.

## Testing windows & district obligations — §74.24(a)

- **(a)(1)** — "A school district must provide at least one window to test between
  January 1 and March 31, one window to test between April 1 and June 30, one window to
  test between July 1 and September 30, and one window to test between October 1 and
  December 31 annually…" (4 windows/year; a specific exam may be taken only once per
  window).
- **(a)(3)** — "A school district shall not charge for an examination for acceleration
  for each primary school grade level or for credit for secondary school academic
  subjects." (No fee.)
- **(a)(4)** — "A school district must have the approval of the school district board of
  trustees to develop its own tests or to purchase examinations…"

## Acceleration in Grades 1–5 — §74.24(b)

- **(b)(2)** — A student in Grades 1–5 must be accelerated one grade upon scoring **80%**
  on a criterion-referenced test for each of language arts/reading, mathematics, science,
  and social studies, **plus** a district representative's recommendation **and** written
  parent/guardian approval.

## Secondary courses (Grades 6–12) — §74.24(c)

- **(c)(1)** — The board of trustees must approve, for each high school course "to the
  extent available, at least four examinations." Must include College Board **AP** and
  **CLEP** exams.
- **(c)(2)–(4)** — Certification/validity/security criteria for an exam (covers all
  assessable TEKS; not publicly published; secure standardized administration; externally
  validated for EOC courses).
- **(c)(8)(C)** — Credit **without prior instruction** requires **80%** "on any other
  criterion-referenced test approved by the school district board of trustees for the
  applicable course."
- **(c)(9)** — "A student may not attempt to earn credit by examination for a specific
  high school course more than two times." (**2-attempt limit**.)
- **(c)(10)** — "If a student fails to earn credit by examination for a specific high
  school course before the beginning of the school year in which the student would
  ordinarily be required to enroll in that course in accordance with the school
  district's prescribed course sequence, the student must satisfactorily complete the
  course…" (Fail the exam → take the class to earn the credit.)
- **(c)(11)** — If credit is earned on an exam scored **80% or higher** (no prior
  instruction), the district "must enter the examination score on the student's
  transcript, and the student is not required to take an applicable end-of-course
  assessment instrument for the course." (**EOC-exempt** on the 80% path.)
- **(c)(12)** — **With prior instruction:** a secondary student "may be given credit for
  an academic subject in which he or she had some prior instruction if the student scores
  **70%** on a criterion-referenced test approved by the school district board of
  trustees" — subject to **local policy**. (On this path the student **still owes the
  STAAR EOC** for graduation — see CBE FAQ #21.)

## Related rule — prerequisites & course sequence (19 TAC §74.11)

- **§74.11(j)** — A student may not be enrolled in a course with a required prerequisite
  unless (1) he has **successfully completed the prerequisite course(s)**, (2) he has
  **demonstrated equivalent knowledge as determined by the district**, or (3) he
  transferred in mid-course from out-of-state/country or a Texas nonpublic school.
- Math prerequisites are **course-specific, not a single chain** (per the Math
  Prerequisite Tables): **Algebra II** requires only **Algebra I**; **Geometry** requires
  only **Algebra I** (so Geometry and Algebra II are parallel — Geometry is *not* a
  prerequisite for Algebra II); **Precalculus** requires **all three — Algebra I,
  Geometry, and Algebra II.** See the downloaded
  [`tea-mathematics-prerequisite-tables.pdf`](./tea-mathematics-prerequisite-tables.pdf).
